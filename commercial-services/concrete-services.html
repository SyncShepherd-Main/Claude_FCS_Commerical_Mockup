Common Questions

Commercial Concrete Services FAQs

What concrete thickness is required for commercial applications?−
Commercial concrete thickness varies by application. Sidewalks typically require 4-5 inches, parking lots 5-6 inches with reinforcement, and loading areas 6-8+ inches with heavy reinforcement. We design concrete specifications based on the expected loads and traffic for each area.
Can you match existing concrete on my property?+
We do our best to match existing concrete in terms of finish, color, and texture. While perfect color matching with aged concrete isn't always possible, we can apply techniques to minimize visible differences. We'll discuss expectations during the proposal process.
How do you handle ADA compliance requirements?+
We're familiar with ADA requirements for commercial concrete including slope requirements, detectable warning surfaces, cross-slope limits, and accessible route widths. We ensure all concrete work meets current ADA standards to protect your property from compliance issues.
Can concrete work be done without closing my parking lot?+
Yes. We typically work in phases, closing only the section being poured while keeping the rest of the lot open. We schedule pours to minimize peak-hour impact and provide temporary striping and signage to direct traffic during construction.
What is the typical timeline for commercial concrete projects?+
Timeline depends on scope. Small projects like ADA ramp corrections can be completed in 1-2 days. Larger projects like parking lot sections typically take 1-2 weeks. Full parking lot replacements may take 3-4 weeks with phased scheduling. Concrete requires 7 days minimum cure time before heavy traffic.
