Common Questions

Office Building FAQs

Can repairs be done outside business hours? −
Absolutely. We regularly perform office building repairs during evenings, nights, and weekends to avoid disrupting your tenants' business operations. Our crews are experienced in working quietly and efficiently during off-hours, and we ensure all work areas are clean and ready for business each morning.
How do you minimize disruption to tenants? +
We use several strategies: scheduling work during off-hours, completing work in phases to minimize affected areas, maintaining clean and professional work sites, providing advance notice to affected tenants, and ensuring common areas are always accessible. Most tenants don't even realize work is being done.
Will foundation repair affect my building's HVAC or other systems? +
Foundation movement can affect various building systems over time. As part of our evaluation, we assess the impact on HVAC, plumbing, and electrical systems. We coordinate with your facilities team and contractors to address any related issues, and our repairs help prevent further system impacts.
Do you provide documentation for property transactions? +
Yes, we provide comprehensive documentation including detailed inspection reports, engineering assessments, before/after photos, and warranty certificates. Our reports are formatted to meet the requirements of commercial real estate transactions, lender due diligence, and lease negotiations.
What's the typical timeline for an office building project? +
Timeline varies based on building size and scope, but most office building projects can be completed in 2-6 weeks. After-hours scheduling may extend the calendar timeline but eliminates business disruption. We'll provide a detailed schedule after your free evaluation.
How does foundation repair impact property value? +
Properly repaired foundations with comprehensive documentation and a lifetime warranty can actually enhance property value by eliminating buyer concerns and providing long-term protection. Our documentation helps you demonstrate the quality of repairs to potential buyers, lenders, and tenants.
